ABOUT PERU

The rich culinary heritage behind one half of Mezze Mio

About

Peruvian cuisine is known for its depth, diversity, and strong connection to geography. Because Peru stretches along the Pacific coast, seafood has always played an important role in its food culture. Fresh fish, citrus, peppers, and herbs come together in dishes that feel bright, bold, and full of life.

Over time, Peruvian cuisine has been shaped by Indigenous traditions as well as Spanish, African, Asian, and other immigrant influences. This blending of cultures helped create one of the most distinctive food traditions in the world, where ingredients, technique, and history all meet on the plate.

For us, Peru is also personal. Callao, the historic port city next to Lima, is especially meaningful because it is where my girlfriend was raised. As a coastal city with a strong maritime identity, Callao reflects the deep connection between Peruvian culture and the sea, making it a powerful inspiration for the Mezze Mio concept.

PERUVIAN CUISINE

Fresh seafood, vibrant citrus, layered cultural influences, and coastal tradition make Peruvian cuisine one of the most exciting culinary identities in the world.

CALLAO

Callao is one of Peru’s most historically important coastal cities and serves as a gateway between the country and the Pacific. Its port, culture, and strong connection to the sea make it an important symbol of Peruvian identity and a personal point of inspiration for our restaurant concept.

COASTAL INFLUENCE

The Peruvian coast has shaped a cuisine built around freshness, brightness, and bold flavor. Seafood dishes, citrus marinades, peppers, and herbs all reflect a culinary tradition that feels vibrant and refined, qualities that inspire the Peruvian side of Mezze Mio.
