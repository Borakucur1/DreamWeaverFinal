ABOUT TÜRKİYE

The culinary heritage behind the other half of Mezze Mio

About

Turkish cuisine is deeply rooted in the traditions of the Ottoman Empire, which brought together flavors, ingredients, and techniques from across Europe, the Middle East, and Central Asia. This historical fusion created a rich and diverse culinary identity that continues to influence food culture today.

Many dishes that are now enjoyed around the world have origins in Türkiye. Foods such as dolma, baklava, and yogurt are central to Turkish cuisine but have also become staples across multiple cultures, reflecting the country’s historical influence and geographic position as a bridge between regions.

Beyond its cuisine, Türkiye is also one of the most visited countries in the world. From the streets of Istanbul to the coastal regions along the Mediterranean, the country attracts millions of visitors each year who come to experience its food, history, and vibrant culture elements that continue to inspire the vision behind Mezze Mio.

TURKISH CUISINE

Rich flavors, centuries of tradition, and globally recognized dishes define Turkish cuisine as one of the most influential culinary cultures in the world.

OTTOMAN INFLUENCE

The Ottoman Empire played a major role in shaping Turkish cuisine, bringing together ingredients and cooking techniques from a wide range of cultures. This legacy created a refined and diverse food tradition that still defines modern Turkish dishes today.

ICONIC FOODS

From sweet baklava to savory dolma and the globally recognized yogurt, Turkish cuisine has influenced countless regions. These dishes represent both tradition and versatility, making them central to the identity of Turkish food culture.
